# Focana Creator Kit

Everything you need to run the creator pilot. Copy-paste ready. All copy here is copy-rule-clean (see `CLAUDE.md`): "buddy," "busy brains," "focus system," zero "beta," no clinical claims. Owned by `@gtm-strategist` — see the plan in `gtm.md`.

> **Golden rule:** gift first, pitch second. Your first message asks nothing except "want to try it free?" The partnership conversation only happens *after* they've used it and like it. This is what earns authentic advocacy instead of a stiff sponsored read.

## 2. One-pager (what to tell a creator)

**Focana — the desktop focus buddy for busy brains.**

Focana is one calm focus system that replaces the patchwork of apps and willpower ADHD brains usually juggle. It stays **in view** while you work — your task, a gentle timer, and a place to catch stray thoughts, all in one window that doesn't vanish when you switch tabs.

**What's inside (one integrated system, not five apps):**
- **Stays in view** — your task and timer never disappear behind other windows.
- **Gentle check-ins** — a quiet "Still focused?" nudge, never a nag.
- **Parking lot** — dump a stray thought mid-task without losing your place.
- **Session history** — pick up right where you left off.
- **Offline, no account, no cloud** — it just works.

**Why it exists:** built by our founder, who has ADHD, after years of hacking together five different tools just to stay on task.

**The offer for you:**
- Free **lifetime** account, no strings.
- A **30-day free trial code** for your audience (vs. the usual 7).
- A **referral bounty** on upgrades through your code.

**Platforms:** macOS today. Windows on the way.

## 3. Talking points (for their content)

Give these as *ideas*, never a script.

- **Start with your real workflow:** "Here's how I actually keep myself on task."
- **Show the moat:** one window that stays put — task + timer + parking lot together, instead of five apps you forget to open.
- **The two "aha" moments worth filming:**
  1. The window staying visible *through* a tab-switch.
  2. The parking lot catching a stray thought mid-task so you don't chase it.
- **The founder angle (optional, powerful):** "made by someone with ADHD, for busy brains."
- **The vibe:** it celebrates finishing instead of guilt-tripping you.

## 4. Do / Don't card

**✅ Do**
- Show how *you* use it, in your own words.
- Disclose the partnership: `#ad` / `#sponsored` / `#gifted` (FTC — required).
- Keep it warm and honest.

**❌ Don't**
- Don't say "clinically validated," "expert-reviewed," "doctor-recommended," or "treats/reduces ADHD." *(Not cleared — will be corrected.)*
- Don't use shame hooks ("if you're a distracted mess…") or the phrase "distracted minds."
- Don't invent urgency ("only today!") — no fake scarcity.
- Don't over-promise outcomes it doesn't deliver.

## 5. Offer & tracking (internal — for you, not the creator)

| Lever | Detail |
|---|---|
| Gift | Lifetime license, sent on first positive reply |
| Audience code | Unique per creator → 30-day free trial |
| Bounty | Per paid upgrade through their code (set one flat number) |
| Flat fee | Phase 2 only, for creators who proved conversion |
| Disclosure | `#ad`/`#sponsored`/`#gifted` required — verify before it posts |

**Tracking:** one **promo code per creator** (codes beat links for a download product). Judge each creator on the funnel that matters — **trial → activation (first completed focus session) → paid** — not views. A creator who drives trials that never activate is a bad fit; cut, don't scale.
